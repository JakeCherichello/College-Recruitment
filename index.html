"Sacred Heart University, founded in 1963 in Bridgeport, Connecticut by the Most Reverend Walter W. Curtis, Bishop of the Diocese of Bridgeport, is distinguished as the first Catholic university in America to be led and staffed entirely by laypeople. Rooted in the liberal arts and Catholic intellectual traditions, the university blends contemporary thinking with a commitment to educating the whole person and preparing students for leadership and service in today's world. For the 2023-2024 academic year, Sacred Heart University has a diverse student body of 11,179, including 6,532 full-time undergraduates, 428 part-time undergraduates, and 4,219 graduate students. The freshman class was selected from a competitive pool of 14,284 applications. The student body is geographically diverse, with representation from 43 states, the District of Columbia, two U.S. territories, and over 40 countries. The university boasts a vibrant alumni network of 56,822 living graduates, among whom are 1,169 married couples and 1,522 with siblings who also graduated from SHU. The alumni community spans 69 countries worldwide."-SHU.edu
