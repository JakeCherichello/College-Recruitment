Sacred Heart University

"Sacred Heart University, founded in 1963 in Bridgeport, Connecticut by the Most Reverend Walter W. Curtis, Bishop of the Diocese of Bridgeport, is distinguished as the first Catholic university in America to be led and staffed entirely by laypeople. Rooted in the liberal arts and Catholic intellectual traditions, the university blends contemporary thinking with a commitment to educating the whole person and preparing students for leadership and service in today's world. For the 2023-2024 academic year, Sacred Heart University has a diverse student body of 11,179, including 6,532 full-time undergraduates, 428 part-time undergraduates, and 4,219 graduate students. The freshman class was selected from a competitive pool of 14,284 applications. The student body is geographically diverse, with representation from 43 states, the District of Columbia, two U.S. territories, and over 40 countries. The university boasts a vibrant alumni network of 56,822 living graduates, among whom are 1,169 married couples and 1,522 with siblings who also graduated from SHU. The alumni community spans 69 countries worldwide." - SHU.edu

General Information

Sacred Heart University is located at 5151 Park Ave, Fairfield, CT 06825, and can be contacted through email sfa@sacredheart.edu and phone 203-371-7980. Its main purpose is to educate students in a safe environment while preparing them for a future career. 100% of students are employed or pursuing further schooling after graduation.

Academic Programs

Our core curriculum builds skills in communication, critical thinking, scientific reasoning, and social awareness, setting the stage for meaningful personal growth. Each staff member is an expert in their field and is committed to the growth of their students.

Student Life

It's lively when you're in the mood for energy and camaraderie, and relaxing when you need to unwind. And there is always something to do, whether it's cheering on the Pioneers, giving your time and talent to a service project, or just hanging out with fun, interesting people at a social event. There are many Greek life opportunities and over 1700 clubs. There is something for everyone at this school!

Cost and Financial Aid

At Sacred Heart University, the total cost is $67,950. The net price is the average cost of the university after aid and scholarship funds are discounted from the total cost, which comes in at $47,500 for the average student receiving need-based aid. Merit scholarships range from $6,000 to $21,000. Programs include: President's Excellence Award, Trustee Scholarship.
